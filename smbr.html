Making Process:

step-1:

  • Soak tamarind in water to rehydrate it.
  • Blend the tamarind with water to create a smooth pulp.
  • Strain the pulp to remove any solids and achieve a desired consistency.

step-2:

  • Rinse and soak split pigeon peas (toor dal) overnight.
  • Drain and cook the lentils in water until they are tender.
  • Mash the cooked lentils to desired consistency.

step-3:

  • Choose a variety of vegetables, such as drumsticks, potatoes, carrots, and onions.
  • Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
  • Saute the vegetables in oil until they are slightly browned.

step-4:

  • Dry roast a blend of spices, including mustard seeds, turmeric, and sambar powder.
  • Grind the roasted spices into a fine powder.

step-5:

  • Combine the cooked lentils, saut√©ed vegetables, tamarind pulp, and spice blend in a pot.
  • Add water to achieve the desired consistency.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the flavors have melded together.

step-6:

  • Add salt and any additional seasonings as desired.
  • Serve the sambar hot with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada.
Variations and Tips
  1. Different regions in South India have their own variations of sambar, using unique combinations of vegetables and spices.
  2. Some recipes may use coconut or coconut milk for added richness and creaminess.
  3. To make sambar more authentic, use traditional South Indian spices and ingredients, such as sambar powder and curry leaves.
  4. Experiment with different vegetables and spices to create your own unique sambar recipe.
